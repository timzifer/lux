# Licensing

lux is available under a **dual-license model**.

## Noncommercial use — free

You may use, modify, and distribute lux free of charge for any
**noncommercial purpose** under the
[PolyForm Noncommercial License 1.0.0](LICENSE), including:

- Personal projects, hobby apps, and private experiments.
- Research, teaching, academic work, and public knowledge sharing.
- Use by nonprofit organizations, public institutions, and
  educational entities.

The full text of the noncommercial license, including the exact
definitions of *noncommercial purpose* and *personal use*, is in
[`LICENSE`](LICENSE).

## Commercial use — paid license

Any commercial use of lux (including, without limitation, use in
products, services, or internal tooling of a for-profit entity that
supports revenue-generating activity) requires a separate commercial
license from the copyright holder.

A commercial license grants:

- The right to use, modify, and distribute lux as part of commercial
  products and services.
- Freedom from the noncommercial restrictions of PolyForm NC.
- Optional support and priority issue handling (depending on tier).

### How to obtain a commercial license

The commercial licensing portal is **being prepared**. Until it goes
live, please open a [GitHub Discussion][discussions] titled
`Commercial license inquiry` with a short description of your
intended use (company, product, scale). We will follow up privately
to negotiate terms.

[discussions]: https://github.com/timzifer/lux/discussions

### What counts as commercial?

If you are unsure whether your use case is commercial, open a
Discussion and ask. When in doubt, the
[PolyForm Noncommercial 1.0.0 text](LICENSE) is authoritative — the
sections **Noncommercial Purposes**, **Personal Uses**, and
**Noncommercial Organizations** are the relevant definitions.

## Contributions

Contributions to lux are accepted under a Contributor License
Agreement (CLA). By opening a pull request you agree to the terms
documented in [`.github/CLA.md`](.github/CLA.md), which grants the
project the rights needed to offer lux under both the noncommercial
and commercial licenses. See [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md)
for the mechanics.
